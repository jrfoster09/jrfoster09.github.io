// Overview

My A-Level Design & Technology Non-Examined Assessment centres on the design and manufacture of a height-adjustable lectern for King Edward VI Grammar School. Commissioned by the school's headteacher, the brief requires a product that can be comfortably used by the full range of people who might stand at it — from the smallest Year 7 student through to tall adults — while also incorporating accessibility provisions for disabled users.

This is a real client project with a real end user, which means the design process must be grounded in genuine anthropometric research, user consultation, and a considered manufacturing specification rather than an abstract exercise.

// Design Thinking

Walnut is currently the leading material candidate — its rich grain, structural density, and aesthetic quality make it well-suited to a piece of furniture intended for a formal school environment. A lectern needs to feel considered and substantial; cheap materials would undermine the authority of whoever is standing at it.

The adjustability mechanism is the central design challenge. It must be intuitive enough to operate quickly in front of an audience, reliable enough to hold a fixed position under load, and robust enough to survive years of school use. Research into existing adjustable furniture systems — including sit-stand desks and architectural lecterns — is informing the mechanism approach.

Accessibility is not an afterthought in this design but a core parameter from the outset. This means considering surface angle, approach clearance for wheelchair users, and control reachability — requirements that intersect with and shape the structural design rather than being bolted on afterwards.
